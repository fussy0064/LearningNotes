Module 01 · Virtual Reality
Virtual Reality — Features & Development Examples

VR creates a fully synthetic, immersive digital environment that replaces the real world. The user wears a headset that renders a 360° scene, tracking every head movement in real time.

Core Features

  • 🥽
    360° Stereoscopic DisplayTwo offset images (one per eye) create realistic 3D depth perception at 90+ FPS to prevent motion sickness.
  • 📡
    6-DoF Head Tracking6 Degrees of Freedom — tracks rotation (pitch, yaw, roll) AND position (left/right, up/down, forward/back) simultaneously.
  • 🎵
    Spatial/Binaural AudioSound appears to come from specific 3D positions, reinforcing the sense of physical presence.
  • 🤲
    Hand/Controller TrackingUsers interact with virtual objects naturally via motion controllers or optical hand-tracking cameras.
  • ⚡
    Haptic FeedbackVibration and resistance in controllers simulate the sensation of touching or holding virtual objects.

Development Examples

Module 01 · VR
VR — Pros & Cons
✅ Advantages
  • Total Immersion: Users feel physically present inside content — unmatched engagement.
  • Safe Training: Practice dangerous scenarios (surgery, flight, military) at zero real-world risk or cost.
  • Enhanced Learning: Studies show 3D spatial learning improves retention by up to 75% over 2D.
  • Emotional Impact: VR experiences generate genuine emotional responses — used in empathy training and therapy.
  • Remote Collaboration: Teams meet in virtual spaces regardless of physical location.
  • Scalable Simulation: One VR setup can replicate thousands of different scenarios cheaply.
❌ Disadvantages
  • High Cost: Quality headsets (Meta Quest Pro, HTC Vive) cost $500–$3,500 each.
  • Motion Sickness: Latency above 20ms causes nausea in many users — especially beginners.
  • Physical Isolation: Completely cuts user off from real environment — safety and social concern.
  • Limited Session Time: Eye strain and discomfort limit comfortable use to 20–30 min sessions.
  • Content Gap: High-quality VR content is expensive and time-consuming to produce.
  • Hardware Requirements: High-end PC VR needs powerful GPU (RTX 3070+) for smooth rendering.
💡
VR Development Tools

Unity and Unreal Engine are the dominant VR development platforms. SDKs include OpenXR (cross-platform standard), Meta SDK (Quest), SteamVR SDK, and WebXR API for browser-based VR.

Module 01 · Augmented Reality
Augmented Reality — Features & Development Examples

AR overlays digital content onto the real world. Users keep full awareness of their surroundings while digital objects, data, or animations are composited on top through a phone, glasses, or headset.

Core Features

  • 📷
    Computer Vision / SLAMSimultaneous Localisation and Mapping — device understands 3D space, detects planes, walls, and surfaces in real time.
  • 🎯
    World AnchoringDigital objects are pinned to real-world positions — they stay fixed when the camera moves.
  • 💡
    Lighting EstimationAR engines sample real-world light conditions and apply matching lighting to virtual objects for realistic blending.
  • 🎭
    Face / Object TrackingDetect and track faces (Snapchat filters), hands (gesture control), or specific marker images.
  • 🌐
    WebARAR delivered through a web browser via WebXR API — no app installation required.

Development Examples

Place true-to-scale 3D furniture models anywhere in your room before purchasing. Built on ARKit with LiDAR scanning.
🎮
Pokémon GO
350M+ downloads. AR overlays virtual Pokémon onto real streets using GPS and camera. Redefined mobile gaming.
🏥
AccuVein
Projects real-time venous map onto patient's skin to help nurses locate veins for injections — 45% fewer failures.
👻
Snapchat Lenses
Real-time face-tracking AR filters using ML-based facial landmark detection. 6 billion Lens plays per day.
Module 01 · AR
AR — Pros & Cons
✅ Advantages
  • No Isolation: Users remain aware of real environment — much safer for mobile use and work settings.
  • Universal Reach: Billions of AR-capable smartphones via ARKit (iOS) and ARCore (Android) — no special hardware.
  • Enhanced Decision Making: Overlay specifications, warnings, or instructions directly onto real objects (factory, medicine).
  • Retail Impact: Virtual try-on increases purchase confidence; IKEA saw 35% fewer returns with AR placement.
  • Real-Time Data: Navigate, measure, identify objects — all overlaid instantly onto what you see.
  • Low Barrier to Entry: WebAR runs in browsers; filter-based AR requires no developer setup.
❌ Disadvantages
  • Limited Field of View: Phone screens show AR in a small frame — smart glasses are expensive and nascent.
  • Battery Drain: Real-time CV, rendering, and GPS heavily drain mobile batteries.
  • Privacy Concerns: Always-on cameras and location tracking raise serious data privacy issues.
  • Occlusion Problems: Virtual objects pass through real ones — complex to fix, requires depth sensors.
  • Sunlight Visibility: Screen-based AR is nearly unusable in bright sunlight.
  • Dependent on Tracking: Loses object placement when camera loses the surface or marker.
🛠️
AR Development Platforms

ARKit (Apple iOS), ARCore (Google Android), Vuforia (marker-based), 8th Wall (WebAR), Snap Lens Studio (social AR), Microsoft Mixed Reality Toolkit (HoloLens)

Module 03 · Live Streaming
Live Streaming — How It Works

Live streaming transmits video in real time across the internet. Unlike on-demand streaming, content is generated and delivered simultaneously — there is no pre-recorded file.

Key Technical Concepts

  • 📸
    Capture & EncodingCamera captures frames → encoder (OBS, Streamlabs) compresses to H.264/H.265 at target bitrate (e.g. 6000 kbps).
  • 📤
    RTMP ProtocolReal-Time Messaging Protocol sends the encoded stream from encoder to ingest server (Twitch/YouTube).
  • 🌐
    CDN DistributionContent Delivery Network splits stream to servers worldwide, minimising latency for viewers in different regions.
  • 🔀
    HLS / DASHStream is segmented into small chunks (2–6 seconds). Viewers' players request next chunk continuously.
  • 📊
    Adaptive BitratePlayer monitors download speed and switches quality tier (1080p → 720p → 480p) automatically to prevent stalling.
✅ Pros of Live Streaming
  • Real-time audience interaction — live chat, Q&A, polls
  • Creates urgency and FOMO (fear of missing out)
  • Authentic, unedited — builds trust and community
  • No post-production cost or time
  • Global reach — viewers from any country
  • Monetisable immediately via donations and subs
❌ Cons of Live Streaming
  • No editing — mistakes are permanent and public
  • Requires stable high-upload internet (>6 Mbps)
  • Technical failures (crash, freeze) visible to viewers
  • Latency (5–30s) makes true real-time interaction delayed
  • Limited discoverability after stream ends
Module 03 · Buffering
Buffering — What It Is & Why It Happens

Buffering is the process of pre-loading a portion of a video stream into memory before playback — ensuring smooth playback by creating a safety buffer against network fluctuations.

Buffer Status Visualisation
Loaded Buffering Not loaded

Causes of Buffering

  • 📶
    Slow Internet ConnectionDownload speed below the required bitrate for the selected quality (e.g. 4K needs 25+ Mbps).
  • 🌐
    Network CongestionPeak hours (evenings) overload ISP infrastructure, reducing effective bandwidth for all users.
  • 📡
    Wi-Fi InterferenceWalls, microwaves, neighbouring networks, and distance from router reduce wireless throughput.
  • 🖥️
    Device PerformanceSlow CPU/GPU cannot decode high-resolution video fast enough, creating a software bottleneck.
  • 🏢
    Server OverloadStreaming servers overwhelmed during popular live events (sports finals, premieres) — CDN mitigates this.

Solutions to Buffering

  • 📉
    Reduce Quality SettingSwitch from 4K/1080p to 720p or 480p. Adaptive bitrate does this automatically.
  • 🔌
    Use Wired ConnectionEthernet cable provides consistent, lower-latency connection vs. Wi-Fi.
  • 🔄
    Close Background AppsOther applications consuming bandwidth (downloads, cloud sync) compete with streaming.
  • 🌐
    CDN OptimisationPlatforms route traffic through nearest CDN edge server to minimise round-trip time.
  • ⏸️
    Pre-Buffer TechniquePause stream for 30 seconds to build up buffer, then resume smooth playback.

Module 04 · CMS
CMS — Pros & Cons
✅ Advantages
  • No Coding Required: Non-technical staff manage content independently — saves developer time and cost.
  • Rapid Deployment: Launch a full website in hours using themes and plugins vs. weeks of custom development.
  • Collaboration: Multiple editors work simultaneously with role-based access and workflow approvals.
  • SEO-Friendly: Built-in tools for metadata, sitemaps, structured data — improves search rankings automatically.
  • Scalability: From personal blog to enterprise platform — same CMS scales with plugins and hosting upgrades.
  • Security Updates: Core, theme, and plugin updates patch vulnerabilities automatically.
❌ Disadvantages
  • Performance Overhead: CMS layers add database queries and PHP processing — slower than static HTML without optimisation.
  • Plugin Conflicts: Too many plugins create compatibility issues, security vulnerabilities, and site crashes.
  • Limited Customisation: Pre-built themes restrict unique design — deep customisation requires developer skills.
  • Security Target: WordPress powers 43% of the web — makes it a prime target for automated attacks.
  • Vendor Lock-In: Content structure tied to platform — migrating CMS is complex and costly.
  • Learning Curve: Enterprise CMS (AEM, Sitecore) require significant training time.

Module 05 · Web Browser
Web Browser — Features & How It Works

A web browser is an application that retrieves, renders, and displays web content. It interprets HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to build the visual interface users see.

How a Browser Renders a Page

  • 1️⃣
    DNS LookupBrowser resolves domain name (google.com) to an IP address via DNS servers.
  • 2️⃣
    TCP/TLS HandshakeEstablishes encrypted connection to server. HTTPS adds TLS layer for security.
  • 3️⃣
    HTTP RequestBrowser sends GET request for the HTML document.
  • 4️⃣
    Parse HTML → DOMBrowser parses HTML and builds Document Object Model tree.
  • 5️⃣
    CSSOM + Render TreeCSS parsed into CSSOM. Combined with DOM to create Render Tree.
  • 6️⃣
    Layout → PaintCalculate positions, then paint pixels to screen. JavaScript runs and can modify DOM at any stage.

Module 05 · Web Hosting
Web Hosting — Types & Examples

Web hosting is a service that stores website files on a server connected to the internet, making the site accessible to anyone with a browser and the domain name.

🏠
Shared Hosting
Multiple websites share one server's resources (CPU, RAM). Cheapest option — $2–$10/month. Performance affected by neighbours. Best for: small blogs, portfolios. Examples: Bluehost, Hostinger, SiteGround.
🏢
VPS Hosting
Virtual Private Server — dedicated slice of a physical server. Isolated resources, root access, fully configurable. $15–$100/month. Best for: growing apps, e-commerce. Examples: DigitalOcean, Linode, Vultr.
🖥️
Dedicated Server
Entire physical server for one client. Maximum performance, full control, high cost ($80–$400/month). Best for: high-traffic sites, databases. Examples: Liquid Web, OVHcloud.
☁️
Cloud Hosting
Resources distributed across multiple servers. Scales instantly, pay-per-use, 99.99% uptime SLA. Best for: variable traffic, global apps. Examples: AWS, Google Cloud, Azure.
🌐
CDN + Static Hosting
Static files served from global edge network — ultra-fast, free/cheap, automatic HTTPS. Best for: static sites, JAMstack. Examples: Netlify, Vercel, Cloudflare Pages.
🛒
Managed Hosting
Provider handles all updates, backups, security, and optimisation. Best for: WordPress sites needing zero maintenance. Examples: WP Engine, Kinsta, Flywheel.
✅ Good Hosting = Good Multimedia
  • Fast load times improve user experience and SEO
  • 99.9%+ uptime keeps streams and downloads reliable
  • CDN reduces latency for global users
  • SSL certificates included with modern plans
❌ Poor Hosting Problems
  • Slow server causes video buffering and high bounce rates
  • Shared hosting neighbours crash your site
  • No CDN = slow media for international visitors
  • Missing SSL = browser security warnings deter visitors

Character Count Validation

Character count validation enforces minimum and maximum length on text inputs — preventing too-short usernames, too-long comments, or passwords that don't meet length requirements.
